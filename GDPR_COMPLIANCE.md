# GDPR Compliance Statement

**Last Updated: December 12, 2025**

## GDPR Overview

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) (EU Regulation 2016/679) is a regulation in EU law on data protection and privacy for all individuals within the European Union. OpenStudy Planner is fully committed to GDPR compliance.

## Article 30 - Records of Processing Activities

### Controller Details
- **Name**: OpenStudy Planner Team
- **Representative**: To be appointed in EU if required
- **Contact**: dpo@openstudyplanner.org

### Purposes of Processing
1. **Service Provision** (Article 6(1)(b))
   - Providing academic planning services
   - Managing user accounts
   - Processing user data for service functionality

2. **Legitimate Interest** (Article 6(1)(f))
   - Service improvement through anonymized analytics
   - Security monitoring and fraud prevention
   - System maintenance and optimization

### Categories of Data Subjects
- Students and academic users
- Registered account holders
- Website visitors (minimal data collected)

### Categories of Personal Data
- **Identity Data**: Email address, username (optional)
- **Academic Data**: Courses, assignments, study schedules
- **Technical Data**: Usage patterns (anonymized)
- **Communication Data**: Support requests, feedback

### Data Retention Periods
- **Active Users**: Duration of account plus 2 years
- **Inactive Users**: 2 years after last login
- **Deleted Data**: 30 days retention for technical purposes
- **Analytics**: Anonymized after 90 days

### Recipients of Personal Data
- No third-party data sharing
- No data selling or marketing
- Cloud service providers with GDPR-compliant DPAs
- Sub-processors vetted for compliance

## Data Subject Rights (Articles 15-22)

### Right to be Informed (Article 13 & 14)
- Clear privacy notices provided
- Transparent information about data processing
- Easy-to-understand explanations

### Right of Access (Article 15)
- Users can request their personal data
- Response within 30 days
- Comprehensive data report provided

### Right to Rectification (Article 16)
- Correction of inaccurate data
- Completion of incomplete data
- Easy modification through account settings

### Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten) (Article 17)
- Immediate deletion upon request
- Exceptions only for legal compliance
- Verification of identity required

### Right to Restrict Processing (Article 18)
- Limit processing of specific data
- Maintain data while restricting use
- Clear process for restriction requests

### Right to Data Portability (Article 20)
- Data provided in machine-readable format
- Direct transfer capabilities where possible
- JSON and CSV export options

### Right to Object (Article 21)
- Object to processing based on legitimate interests
- Opt-out of specific processing activities
- No discrimination for exercising rights

### Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making (Article 22)
- No automated decision-making that affects users
- Human oversight for all significant decisions
- Clear explanations for all processes

## Data Protection by Design and Default (Article 25)

### Privacy by Design
- Privacy considered from project inception
- Data minimization principles applied
- Regular privacy impact assessments conducted

### Privacy by Default
- Most private settings as default
- No optional data collection without consent
- Clear opt-in for non-essential features

## Security of Processing (Article 32)

### Technical Measures
- **Encryption**: AES-256 for data at rest
- **Transport Security**: TLS 1.3 for data in transit
- **Access Controls**: Multi-factor authentication
- **Pseudonymization**: Where appropriate
- **Regular Testing**: Security assessments quarterly

### Organizational Measures
- **Staff Training**: Annual GDPR training
- **Access Policies**: Need-to-know basis
- **Incident Response**: 72-hour breach notification
- **Documentation**: Comprehensive compliance records

## Data Breach Management (Article 33 & 34)

### Internal Process
1. Detection and assessment within 24 hours
2. Containment and mitigation
3. Notification to supervisory authority within 72 hours
4. Communication to affected individuals if high risk

### Breach Notification Protocol
- **Contact**: dpo@openstudyplanner.org
- **Hotline**: +1-555-BREACH (273224)
- **Response Time**: Immediate acknowledgment, detailed report within 72 hours

## International Data Transfers (Chapter V)

### Adequacy Decisions
- Transfers within EEA: Fully compliant
- UK: Adequacy decision applies
- Other countries: Standard Contractual Clauses

### Standard Contractual Clauses
- European Commission approved SCCs implemented
- Supplementary measures where required
- Transparency about data locations

## Data Protection Impact Assessments (Article 35)

### Required Assessments
- New feature implementations
- Significant changes to processing
- High-risk processing activities

### DPIA Process
1. Identify necessity and proportionality
2. Assess risks to data subjects
3. Identify mitigation measures
4. Documentation and review
